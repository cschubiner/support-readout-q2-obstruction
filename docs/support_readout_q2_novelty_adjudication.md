# Novelty Adjudication: Support, Readout, And Binary Rank

Status: frozen-package reviewer aid, not a claim of final priority.

## Verdict

The note should not be advertised as a new theorem in locally covariant QFT,
DHR/DR reconstruction, OAQEC, sufficiency theory, or black-hole spectroscopy.
The defensible novelty claim is narrower: these standard reconstructions are
all relative to the algebra, sector category, channel, experiment, or spectral
observable actually supplied. If the supplied data factor through a represented
quotient, then finite central support, kernel status, finite rank, and the
predicate `q=2` are not forced unless an additional non-quotient premise is
added.

This is probably best described as a finite-central classification/no-go
packaging of known descent logic, with a Lean-checked finite kernel. It should
be demoted immediately if a source already states the same support/readout
criterion and binary-rank obstruction in this exact operator-algebra form.

## Reviewer Questions Sharpened

1. Is the finite-central support/readout criterion already known under a
   standard name?
2. Does any standard physical axiom already present in the note's inputs exclude
   hidden finite central completions, or is that axiom an extra positive
   premise?
3. Is the binary step too strong? In particular, can a weaker standard
   hypothesis force `q=2` after support/readout accountability without adding a
   finite-alphabet law or direct `q=2` spectroscopy?

## Current Claim Boundary

The note proves a negative result for quotient-visible data and gives positive
exits. It does not prove that the binary primitive is unavoidable. The honest
answer remains: under the stated quotient-visible assumptions, off-support
finite `q`-factors survive; binary is conditional until support/readout
accountability and an alphabet law or direct binary measurement are derived.
