# Team 1 Positive Accountability Theorem Note

Status: live referee-facing theorem note. This is not a frozen theorem artifact
and does not modify the frozen manuscript, Lean archive, or SHA manifest.

Purpose: state the constructive counterpart to the finite-center residue
lemma. The negative papers show that quotient-visible data do not reconstruct
hidden finite central rank or `q=2`. This note states the exact positive
premises under which finite-center rank is recoverable.

## Standard Facts To Concede

Team 1 should not claim novelty for these ingredients.

1. If `M` is a von Neumann algebra and `I` is an ultraweakly closed two-sided
   `*`-ideal, then `I` is a central-projection summand and `M/I` is the
   complementary summand.
2. For a finite center `Z_F ~= ell_infty(F)`, the atom labels are ordinary
   classical coordinates once the center is included in the represented
   algebra.
3. Blackwell comparison, statistical sufficiency, and Petz sufficiency already
   express when a declared experiment, channel, or subalgebra retains the
   information needed for a stated decision or recovery task.

The possible Team 1 value is narrower: translating the finite-center positive
criterion into black-hole/AQFT reconstruction language and separating it from
unsupported binary-rank claims.

## Finite-Center Accountability Criterion

Let `F` be a finite set with `q = |F|`, and let

```text
    Z_F = ell_infty(F),        V_F = Z_F / C1.
```

Here `V_F` is the finite-center contrast space: constants have been removed,
so `dim V_F = q - 1`.

Let `R : V_F -> W` be the declared readout/accountability map, or the linear
map generated by the declared normal probes, effects, outcome statistics,
sector records, recovery task, modular record, finite-alphabet law, or direct
measurement channel. Define

```text
    N_R = ker R.
```

Then the data visible through `R` determine exactly the quotient

```text
    V_F / N_R
```

and the observable finite-center rank

```text
    rank_R(F) = dim(V_F / N_R) = rank R.
```

Consequently:

1. The full finite central cardinality is recoverable from `R` only if
   `N_R = 0` and an admissibility premise says that every central atom in
   `Z_F` is physical rather than null, gauge, excluded, or an unrepresented
   bookkeeping extension. Under those premises,

   ```text
       q = rank R + 1.
   ```

2. A binary conclusion `q = 2` is recoverable only if the accounted contrast
   rank is one and a finite-alphabet law, direct measurement, maximality
   premise, or admissibility theorem identifies the accounted center as the
   full finite center rather than a coarser quotient of a larger hidden center.

3. If `N_R != 0`, then finite-center completions with different hidden
   cardinalities can induce the same `R`-visible data after quotienting by
   `N_R`. No rule using only the declared `R`-visible data can reconstruct the
   hidden cardinality or the predicate `q=2`.

## Proof Skeleton

The proof is finite-dimensional linear algebra plus the ordinary
central-projection quotient fact.

1. The finite center `ell_infty(F)` has atomic basis `{delta_f : f in F}`.
   Modding out constants gives the contrast space `V_F` of dimension `q-1`.
2. A declared observation family factors through a linear map `R : V_F -> W`.
   Two central contrasts with the same image under `R` are indistinguishable
   for every task built from that observation family.
3. Therefore the declared data determine only the quotient `V_F / ker R`, not
   the discarded kernel directions.
4. If `ker R = 0` and every central atom is admitted as physical, the dimension
   of `V_F` is known and the finite-center cardinality is `dim V_F + 1`.
5. If `ker R != 0`, or if some atoms are null/gauge/excluded/unrepresented,
   one can refine the hidden center inside the null directions without changing
   the declared `R`-visible data. The hidden cardinality and `q=2` are then not
   reconstruction targets of the supplied data.

## Relation To The Negative Notes

This is the positive exit from the finite-center no-go package:

- Paper 1: quotient-visible operational data do not by themselves reconstruct
  hidden finite centers, tensor coordinates, or absolute entropy calibration.
- Paper 2: support/readout data do not by themselves force full ambient
  support, kernel status, finite central rank, or `q=2`.
- This note: finite central rank is recoverable exactly when the supplied
  readout is faithful on the physical finite-center contrast space, and `q=2`
  additionally requires a premise that the accounted one-dimensional contrast
  is the whole finite center rather than a quotient of a larger hidden center.

## Primary Anchors

- Blackwell comparison of experiments: sufficiency is relative to a declared
  experiment or decision task.
- Petz sufficiency: recovery/equality of relative entropy is relative to a
  declared subalgebra or channel.
- Von Neumann algebra ideal theory: ultraweakly closed two-sided ideals are
  central-projection summands.
- BFV LCQFT, Fewster-Verch dynamical locality, DHR/DR reconstruction, and
  JLMS/OAQEC: positive reconstruction theorems reconstruct what their
  hypotheses supply; they do not automatically add a missing finite-center
  accountability premise unless maximality or faithful support is part of the
  hypothesis.
