## Why The Theorem Itself Is Probably Elementary

The proof skeleton is too short to carry a frontier-theorem claim by itself:

1. Normal quotient kernels in von Neumann algebras are central-projection
   summands.
2. Any record that factors through the quotient is constant on the hidden
   central fiber.
3. Targets such as hidden support, finite central rank, and `q=2` vary over
   that fiber.
4. Therefore no reconstruction rule from the quotient-visible record alone can
   determine those targets.

This is a useful obstruction only if the target community tends to overread
positive reconstruction inputs, such as LCQFT/RCE, sector reconstruction,
OAQEC/JLMS recovery, crossed-product entropy, or spectroscopy, as certifying
maximality or binary rank when those hypotheses do not actually supply a
separator or direct finite-alphabet measurement.
